Fever / Pain (IV/IM) 10–15 mg/kg Every 6–8 hours (slow IV) Enter weight 1 g/dose
Indications
Moderate–severe fever refractory to paracetamol/ibuprofen, acute moderate pain, colic pain (biliary, renal). Widely used in Indonesia as 2nd-line antipyretic.
Contraindications
⚠ Age <3 months or <5 kg, known agranulocytosis with prior metamizole, G6PD deficiency, bone marrow suppression, hypersensitivity to pyrazolones. Banned in several countries (US, UK) — use with awareness.
Side Effects
⚠ Agranulocytosis (rare but potentially fatal — 1:1,000,000), anaphylaxis/anaphylactoid reactions (especially IV), hypotension (rapid IV!), rash, nausea. Renal toxicity with prolonged use.
Interactions
Warfarin (↑INR), cyclosporine (↓levels), chlorpromazine (↑hypothermia risk), other NSAIDs (↑GI/renal risk), alcohol (↑side effects)
Clinical Notes
⚠ IV must be given SLOWLY (>15 min) to prevent hypotension and anaphylactoid reactions — never bolus. Commonly used in Indonesian clinical practice for refractory fever. Available: 500 mg/ml drops, 500 mg tablet, 500 mg/ml injection. Not first-line — use paracetamol first. Banned in US/UK/Japan due to agranulocytosis risk.

Indications
Acute severe/life-threatening asthma (IV, when salbutamol/corticosteroids insufficient), COPD exacerbation (adjunct), neonatal apnoea of prematurity, chronic asthma maintenance (oral, now less preferred — use ICS first).
Contraindications
Hypersensitivity to theophylline/ethylenediamine, uncontrolled seizure disorder, active peptic ulcer, porphyria, tachyarrhythmias. Caution: hepatic impairment (↓clearance), cardiac disease, hyperthyroidism, fever (↓clearance). Do NOT give loading dose if patient already on oral theophylline — check level first.
Side Effects
⚠ NARROW THERAPEUTIC INDEX. Dose-related: nausea, vomiting, headache, insomnia, tachycardia, tremor (10–20 mcg/ml). Toxic (>20 mcg/ml): severe vomiting, arrhythmias (SVT, VT), seizures, hypotension, cardiac arrest. Hypokalaemia (at high doses).
Interactions
⚠ MANY interactions — CYP1A2 substrate. ↑Levels: erythromycin, ciprofloxacin, clarithromycin, cimetidine, oral contraceptives, allopurinol, verapamil. ↓Levels: rifampicin, phenytoin, phenobarbital, carbamazepine, smoking. Salbutamol (↑hypokalaemia). Halothane (↑arrhythmia risk).
Clinical Notes
⚠ TDM MANDATORY for IV use. Target serum theophylline: 10–20 mcg/ml (bronchodilation); 7–12 mcg/ml (neonatal apnoea). Aminophylline = theophylline 80% + ethylenediamine 20% — dose as aminophylline salt. IV loading: infuse slowly over 20–30 min minimum — rapid infusion causes fatal arrhythmia. Do NOT give loading dose to patient already on theophylline. Check: heart rate (max 110 bpm during infusion), ECG, electrolytes (K⁺). Available: 250 mg/10ml injection (25 mg/ml); 100 mg, 200 mg tablet; 225 mg slow-release tablet. Widely used in Indonesian hospitals especially for acute asthma.

Interactions

Clinical Notes
